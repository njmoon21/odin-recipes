Chicken Katsu Curry

Chicken Katsu Curry mixes Japanese and Indian cuisine. The protein is a sliced bed of deep-fried, panko-breaded chicken breast, served with extremely savory and mildly spicy curry sauce, all poured over a bed of steamed white rice. The curry sauce is usually simmered with carrots, potatoes, and white onions.

Ingredients - Chicken Katsu

  • 1 Chicken breast
  • Salt & Pepper
  • 1 + 1/2 Tbsp of Flour
  • 1 Beaten Egg
  • 2/3 Cup of Panko Breadcrumbs
  • Oil for Deep Frying

Ingredients - Curry and Rice

  • House Vermont Curry (Medium Hot)
  • Cooked, Steamed White Rice
  • White Onions - Sliced into 1cm wide pieces
  • Potatoes - Cut into 1.5cm cubes
  • Carrots - Sliced into 7mm thick pieces

Steps

  1. Put oil into a pot and saut√© onion pieces until the edges start browning.
  2. Add potato and carrot pieces to the pot and stir.
  3. Add water and bring it to a boil. Remove scum as it rises.
  4. Add curry roux (House Vermont) and cook.
  5. Cook chicken cutlets and slice them.
  6. Put cooked rice on one side of a serving plate, place cutlet pieces next to the rice.
  7. Pour curry on the side next to the cutlet away from the rice.
